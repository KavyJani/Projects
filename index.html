Marketing Analysis On Netflix:

Netflix Data:

Data Requirement:

Subscriber count, revenue, and marketing spend data by region (Asia-Pacific, EMEA, Latin America, US & Canada) from Q1 2018 to Q2 2020, including metrics to calculate customer acquisition cost (CAC) and ARPU.

Data Summary:

Between Q1 2018 and Q2 2020, Asia-Pacific showed the highest growth in subscribers and revenue. Other regions displayed varied trends, with a clear link between lower CAC and stronger profitability across all regions.

Analysis:

I analyzed regional trends in subscriber growth, revenue performance, and CAC using Excel functions like IFERROR, growth rate calculations, and pivot tables. Data cleaning ensured accuracy while visualizations revealed performance disparities and marketing efficiency across the four major global regions.

Solutions:

● Team up with phone and device brands

to offer Netflix bundles, making it easier and cheaper for new users to join without extra effort.

● Try region-specific pricing plans

that match what people can afford, while still nudging them towards better-value subscriptions.

● Invest in shows that speak the local language and culture

helping people feel more connected and encouraging new subscriptions.

● Use smart data models to spot users likely to cancel

and send them timely, personalized offers to bring them back before they leave.

Perfomance Analysis:

Perfomance Analysis:

Data Requirement :

The objective was to evaluate the effectiveness of marketing campaigns across different regions by analyzing revenue, spend, and ROI. This analysis aimed to guide future marketing strategies, identify high-performing regions and channels, and optimize budget allocation for maximum returns.

Data Summary:

The raw dataset contained campaign-wise and region-wise figures for revenue, marketing spend, and campaign type (digital or traditional). It included ROI details, helping to distinguish performance trends across continents and assess the success of each marketing effort.

Analysis:

I cleaned the dataset in Excel by removing duplicates, standardizing numeric fields, and formatting columns. Then, in Power BI, I modeled relationships, applied DAX functions like SUM(), AVERAGE(), and CALCULATE() to compute key metrics, and visualized insights using clustered charts and KPIs.

Solutions:

● Put more budget behind what’s working –

Influencer Marketing and Social Media Ads are delivering great returns, so increasing investment here can boost overall performance.

● Test and tweak digital campaigns –

Run A/B tests to figure out what messages and visuals connect best with your audience, helping refine strategies in real time.

● Focus on top-performing regions –

Direct more marketing efforts toward Africa, Asia, and North America, where your spend is turning into revenue most efficiently.

● Encourage learning across teams –

Share successful tactics from high-performing campaigns like Social Media across other channels (like Email) to lift overall results.

Credit Card Analysis :

Data Summary :

Data Requirement:

The analysis aimed to understand customer behavior, spending patterns, and delinquency trends across demographics and card types. The goal was to identify revenue-driving segments, evaluate credit risk, and develop actionable strategies to improve portfolio performance and reduce financial losses.

Data Summary:

The dataset included customer demographics, card types, income levels, transaction methods, interest earned, and delinquency rates. It offered a detailed view of segment-wise revenue contribution and risk profiles across different customer types and credit card usage behavior.

Analysis:

Using SQL, I cleaned and segmented data by card type, job, and spending. Then, I visualized KPIs like revenue, interest, income, and delinquency in Power BI through interactive dashboards, leveraging filters and visuals to reveal behavior-based insights across customer profiles.

Solutions:

● Reward your top spenders

by offering exclusive perks or personalized upgrade options, they’re already loyal, so make them feel valued.

● Be cautious with riskier segments,

consider offering lower credit limits or customized plans to retirees or lower-income users to reduce default risk.

● Encourage card swiping in stores

by offering instant discounts or cashback, it’s a simple way to boost revenue.

● Create flexible cards

for freelancers and graduates, features like adjustable limits, smart rewards, or even tools for tracking business expenses could really appeal to them.
